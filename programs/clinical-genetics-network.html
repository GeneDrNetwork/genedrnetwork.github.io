Clinical Reference Hub

Genetics & Rare Diseases, IEM (Metabolic Disorder) Management Guidelines is a centralized clinical resource for commonly seen genetic disorders, rare diseases, and inborn errors of metabolism. The program brings together diagnostic pathways, genetic testing recommendations, management protocols, treatment algorithms, monitoring guidance, care pathways, and expert consensus recommendations.

Guideline Sections

Commonly Seen Genetic Disorders

Practical management guidelines for commonly seen genetic disorders, including diagnostic evaluation, genetic testing, surveillance, treatment, referrals, and family counseling.

Rare Diseases

Clinical guidance for rare disease diagnosis and management, including genomic testing pathways, multidisciplinary care, treatment options, surveillance recommendations, and patient-family resources.

Inborn Error of Metabolism (IEM) / Metabolic Disorders

Quick clinical guide for inborn errors of metabolism and metabolic disorders, including emergency evaluation, diagnostic testing, acute management, chronic care, diet therapy, medications, and follow-up recommendations.

IEM / Metabolic Disorder Quick Guide

The Inborn Error of Metabolism (IEM) / Metabolic Disorder Quick Guide is preserved within this combined program as practical education around metabolic red flags, emergency evaluation, initial laboratory workup, timely referral, and coordinated care for patients with suspected inherited metabolic disorders.

  • Clinical red flags for inborn errors of metabolism and metabolic disorders
  • Initial laboratory evaluation, emergency assessment, and urgent triage concepts
  • Referral pathways for genetics, metabolic, and specialty care
  • Acute management, chronic care, diet therapy, medications, and follow-up recommendations
  • Education for clinicians, trainees, families, and community partners
