For patients and caregivers

Patient & Family Education

New to genetics? Begin with these short, plain-language introductions. You do not need a science background.

What is a genetic disorder?

A genetic disorder is a health condition related to a change in a gene or chromosome. Some are inherited, while others happen for the first time in a person.

Video coming soon
What is a chromosome?

A chromosome is a package of DNA inside our cells. Chromosomes carry many genes and usually come in pairs.

Video coming soon
What is a gene?

A gene is a section of DNA that gives the body instructions. Genes can influence how our bodies grow, develop, and work.

Video coming soon
What is genetic testing?

Genetic testing looks for changes in genes or chromosomes that may help explain a health condition or a person's chance of developing one.

Video coming soon
What is chromosomal microarray?

A chromosomal microarray looks for missing or extra pieces of chromosomes. It can help identify certain causes of developmental or health differences.

Video coming soon
What is panel testing?

Panel testing checks a selected group of genes linked to a particular condition or set of symptoms at the same time.

Video coming soon
What is whole exome sequencing?

Whole exome sequencing looks for changes in the parts of genes that provide instructions for making proteins.

Video coming soon
What is whole genome sequencing?

Whole genome sequencing reads nearly all of a person's DNA. It can look for many different types of genetic changes.

Video coming soon
What is a variant of uncertain significance?

This is a genetic change that experts cannot yet classify as harmless or disease-causing. More information may become available over time.

Video coming soon
What does a positive genetic test result mean?

A positive result means the test found a genetic change related to the reason for testing. A genetics professional can explain what it may mean for you and your family.

Video coming soon
What does a negative genetic test result mean?

A negative result means the test did not find the genetic change it was designed to detect. It does not always rule out a genetic cause.
