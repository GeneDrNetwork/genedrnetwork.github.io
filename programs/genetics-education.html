For patients and caregivers

Patient & Family Education

New to genetics? Begin with these short, plain-language introductions. You do not need a science background.

1. What to Expect During Your Child’s First Genetic Clinic Visit?

Learn what happens during a genetics appointment, including review of medical history, family history, physical examination, possible genetic testing recommendations, and discussion of diagnosis and treatment options. Understanding the process can help families prepare for their visit and know what questions to ask.

2. What Is a Genetic Disorder?

A genetic disorder is a condition caused by changes in genes, chromosomes, or DNA. Some genetic conditions are inherited from parents, while others occur for the first time in an individual. Genetics can affect growth, development, metabolism, and many other aspects of health.

3. What Are Genes, DNA, and Chromosomes?

Genes are the body's instructions for growth and function. DNA is the molecule that contains these instructions, and chromosomes are packages of DNA found in nearly every cell. Understanding these basic concepts helps explain how genetic conditions occur.

4. What Is Genetic Testing and What Are the Types of Genetic Testing?

Genetic testing examines DNA to identify changes that may explain medical conditions, guide treatment, or assess inherited risks. Different tests are available depending on the clinical question and suspected diagnosis.

What Is Genetic Testing?

Types of Genetic Testing

5. What Is Chromosomal Microarray?

Chromosomal microarray is a genetic test that looks for missing or extra pieces of chromosomes. It is commonly used to evaluate developmental delays, intellectual disabilities, autism spectrum disorders, congenital anomalies, and other genetic conditions.

6. What Are Whole Exome Sequencing, Whole Genome Sequencing, and Genetic Testing Consent?

Whole Exome Sequencing (WES) analyzes the protein-coding regions of genes, while Whole Genome Sequencing (WGS) analyzes nearly all of a person's DNA. Before testing, families review the benefits, limitations, possible findings, and consent process.

7. How to Read a Genetic Sequencing Test Report and How to Understand Genetic Testing Results?

Genetic test reports contain information about identified variants, their clinical significance, inheritance patterns, and recommendations for follow-up care. Learning how to interpret these reports can help patients and families better understand their results.

8. What Is Gene Therapy?

Gene therapy is an emerging treatment approach that aims to correct, replace, or modify disease-causing genes. It has become an important area of precision medicine and offers new treatment possibilities for certain inherited disorders.
