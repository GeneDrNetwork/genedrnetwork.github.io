For patients and caregivers

Patient & Family Education

New to genetics? Begin with these short, plain-language introductions. You do not need a science background.

What is a chromosome?

A chromosome is a package of DNA inside our cells. Chromosomes carry many genes and usually come in pairs.

Watch YouTube Introduction Coming soon

What is a gene?

A gene is a section of DNA that gives the body instructions. Genes can influence how our bodies grow, develop, and work.

Watch YouTube Introduction Coming soon

What is whole exome sequencing?

Whole exome sequencing looks for changes in the parts of genes that provide instructions for making proteins.

Watch YouTube Introduction Coming soon

What is whole genome sequencing?

Whole genome sequencing reads nearly all of a person's DNA. It can look for many different types of genetic changes.

Watch YouTube Introduction Coming soon

What is a variant of uncertain significance?

This is a genetic change that experts cannot yet classify as harmless or disease-causing. More information may become available over time.

Watch YouTube Introduction Coming soon

What is a genetic disorder?

A genetic disorder is a health condition related to a change in a gene or chromosome. Some are inherited, while others happen for the first time in a person.
